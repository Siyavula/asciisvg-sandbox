Sandbox: Coulomb’s law acting at right angles

Given the following arrangement of charges, where the charges are as follows:

  • \(Q_{A} = \text{3 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}} \ \text{C} \)
  • \(Q_{B} = \text{2 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}} \ \text{C} \)
  • \(Q_{C} = \text{3 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}} \ \text{C} \)

The distance between \(Q_{A}\) and \(Q_{B}\) is \(\text{0,16}\ \text{m}\), while the distance between \(Q_{B}\) and \(Q_{C}\) is \(\text{0,2}\ \text{m}\).

Calculate the magnitude and bearing (relative to the positive x-axis) of the resultant force acting on \(Q_{B}\).

Write the magnitude correct to 3 significant figures. Write the bearing as a positive integer.

Answer:  Magnitude = N, at a bearing of °     [6 marks]

Correct!

STEP [−1 mark ⇒ 5 / 6 marks left]

\(Q_{A}\) and \(Q_{B}\) are like charges, and therefore \(Q_{B}\) will experience a repulsive force. The force pushes \(Q_{B}\) downwards, or in the negative y-direction. This force is shown below as \(F_{A}\).

\(Q_{C}\) and \(Q_{B}\) are like charges, and therefore \(Q_{B}\) will experience a repulsive force. The force pushes \(Q_{B}\) to the left, or in the negative x-direction. This force is shown below as \(F_{C}\).

STEP [−2 marks ⇒ 3 / 6 marks left]

Since we know the direction of the forces, we can ignore the signs of the charges. We will calculate the magnitude of the forces using positive charge values.

\(F_{A}\) can be calulated as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} F_{A} &= \frac{k Q_{A} Q_{B}}{r^2}\\ &= \frac{(\text{9 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{9}})(\text{3 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}})(\text{2 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}})}{(\text{0,16})^\text{2}} \\ &= \text{2,1094 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}}\ \text{N}\\ \end{aligned} \]

Similarly, \(F_{C}\) can be calculated as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} F_{C} &= \frac{k Q_{B} Q_{C}}{r^2}\\ &= \frac{(\text{9 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{9}})(\text{2 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}})(\text{3 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−9}})}{(\text{0,2})^\text{2}} \\ &= \text{1,3500 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}}\ \text{N}\\ \end{aligned} \]

These forces can be drawn as shown below:

STEP [−1 mark ⇒ 2 / 6 marks left]

To calculate the magnitude of the resultant force, we can use Pythagoras:

\[ \begin{aligned} F_{res} &= \sqrt{(F_{A})^2 + (F_{C})^2} \\ &= \sqrt{(\text{2,1094 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}})^2 + (\text{1,3500 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}})^2} \\ &= \sqrt{(\text{4,4495 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−12}}) + (\text{1,8225 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−12}})} \\ &= \sqrt{(\text{6,2720 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−12}})} \\ &= \text{2,5044 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}}\ \text{N}\\ \end{aligned} \]

STEP [−2 marks ⇒ 0 / 6 marks left]

To calculate the bearing of the resultant force, we use trigonometry:

\[ \begin{aligned} \tan(\theta) & = \frac{F_{A}}{F_{C}} \\ \end{aligned} \]

Rearranging to make \(\theta\) the subject:

\[ \begin{aligned} \theta & = \tan^{-1} \left( \frac{F_{A}}{F_{C}} \right) \\ & = \tan^{-1} \left( \frac{\text{2,1094 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}}}{\text{1,3500 } × \text{ 10}^{\text{−6}}} \right) \\ & = \tan^{-1} \left( \text{1,5625}... \right) \\ & = \text{57,38}^{\circ} \\ \end{aligned} \]

Taking into account the quadrant the resultant lies in, we can calculate the bearing of the resultant force relative to the positive x-axis:

\[ \begin{aligned} \theta = \text{237,38}^{\circ} \leftarrow \textrm{relative to the positive x-axis}\\ \end{aligned} \]
