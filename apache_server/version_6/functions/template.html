Sandbox: Emf induced in a solenoid

Given the following solenoid in a magnetic field:

Consider a solenoid. The change in magnetic flux \(\Delta \phi\) of the solenoid is \(\text{23,65}\ \text{Wb}\) in an interval of \(\text{3,7}\ \text{s}\). The emf \(\Large{\varepsilon}\) induced in the coil is \(\text{−83,11}\ \text{V}\).

Determine the number of turns of wire \(N\) on the solenoid. Write your answer correct to the nearest integer.

Answer:  \(N=\) \(\text{turns}\)     [3 marks]

STEP [−2 marks ⇒ 0 / 3 marks left]

We now recall the formula for emf \(\Large{\varepsilon}\):

\[ \begin{aligned} \Large{\varepsilon} & = - N \left( \frac{\Delta \phi}{\Delta \textrm{t}} \right) \\ \end{aligned} \]

Substituting into the equation:

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{−83,11} & = - (N) \left( \frac{\text{23,65}}{\text{3,7}} \right) \\ \end{aligned} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} N & = - \frac {(\text{−83,11})(\text{3,7})}{\text{23,65}} \\ \\ \end{aligned} \]

Solving we get:

\[ \begin{aligned} N & = \text{13}\ \text{turns}\\ \end{aligned} \]
