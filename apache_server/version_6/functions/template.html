Sandbox: Converting decimal numbers to fractions

Write \(\text{0,}\dot{8}\) in the form \(\frac{a}{b}\) (where \(a\) and \(b\) are integers that share no factors). In other words, show that \(\text{0,}\dot{8}\) is a rational number.

Answer:      [3 marks]    

Help! How should I type my answer?

You are expected to write a fraction here.

You can write pure fractions using the division symbol: / For example, to write the fraction \(-\frac{2}{3}\) the correct input would be: -2/3

You can also write mixed numbers by first writing the integer part, then a space and then the fraction part. For example, to write \(2 \frac{1}{8}\) the correct input would be: 2 1/8

Correct!

This question involves a rather "messy" number: \( \text{0,}\dot {8} \) is equal to \( \text{0,8888} \ldots\) it goes on forever!! To answer this question, we will do something clever to tidy up that long (neverending) mess of decimals: we are going to multiply by 10. The reason is because there is a single decimal point repeating . It turns out that if we multiply by 10 and then subtract as shown below, all of the decimals will cancel, which will tidy up that long decimal number.

First, let \(x = \text{0,8888} \ldots\)
Next, multiply both sides of the equation above by 10 to get: \(\text{10}x = \text{8,8888} \ldots\)

Now subtract the two equations. Notice that when we do this, the repeating digits cancel each other, and we end up with an integer. \[ \begin{aligned} \text{10}x & = \; \; \; \text{8,8888} \ldots \\ - x & = - \text{0,8888} \ldots \\ \hline \\ \text{9}x & = \; \; \; \text{8,0000}\ldots \quad \longleftarrow \; ^{\textrm{The decimal values all cancel,}}_{\textrm{leaving a nice, tidy integer!}}\\ \text{9}x & = \; \; \; \text{8} \\ \therefore x & = \frac{\text{8}}{\text{9}} \end{aligned} \]
